If you want to start developing a cultural taste and increase your knowlegde about several drinks, all with their own interesting characteristics, you are at the right place! Why do you want to develop a taste of your "own"? One reason could be for social drinking. Social drinking, also commonly referred to as responsible drinking, refers to casual drinking of alcoholic beverages in a social setting without an intent to become intoxicated. In Western cultures, good news is often celebrated by a group of people having a few alcoholic drinks. For example, alcoholic drinks may be served to "wet the baby's head" in the celebration of a birth. Buying someone an alcoholic drink is often considered a gesture of goodwill. It may be an expression of gratitude, or it may mark the resolution of a dispute.

Tastings

Beer

Beer is one of the oldest and most widely consumed alcoholic drinks in the world, and the third most popular drink overall after water and tea. Most modern beer is brewed with hops, which add bitterness and other flavours and act as a natural preservative and stabilizing agent.

Wine

Wine is an alcoholic beverage made from grapes fermented without the addition of sugars, acids, enzymes, water, or other nutrients. Wine has been produced for thousands of years and has long played an important role in religion.

Whisky

Whisky - or whiskey - is a type of distilled alcoholic beverage made from fermented grain mash. Various grains (which may be malted) are used for different varieties, including barley, corn (maize), rye, and wheat. It is typically aged in wooden casks, generally made of charred white oak.

We do not serve alcohol below the age of 18 years. Enjoy your drinks responsibly.
